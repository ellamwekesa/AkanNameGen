Get To Know Your Akan Name

The history of Akan names is deeply rooted in the cultural and spiritual beliefs of the Akan people. The naming tradition dates back centuries and is a reflection of their ancestry and identity. The names are not just identifiers; they are living symbols of history, spirituality, and the enduring bond between people, their families, and the divine.
The Akan people of Ghana, Ivory Coast, and Togo frequently name their children after the day of the week they were born and the order in which they were born. These "day names" have further meanings concerning the soul and character of the person. Middle names have considerably more variety and can refer to their birth order, twin status, or an ancestor's middle name.
This naming tradition is shared throughout West Africa and the African diaspora
